# Toll-Free Verification Examples

Real examples of good vs bad form submissions to help you get approved.

---

## Business Name Examples

### GOOD
- Acme Corporation Inc.
- Johnson & Smith Legal Services LLC
- TechStart Solutions Ltd
- Mary's Bakery
- 123 Logistics Corp

### BAD
- acme corp (no capitalization, informal)
- ACME (abbreviation - use full legal name)
- My Business (too generic)
- test company (not a real business name)
- asdfasdf (gibberish)

---

## Website Examples

### GOOD
- https://www.acmecorp.com
- https://acme.co
- https://shop.mybusiness.com

### BAD
- acmecorp.com (missing https://)
- www.acmecorp.com (missing https://)
- http://acmecorp.com (http instead of https - less trusted)
- facebook.com/mybusiness (social media, not business website)
- N/A (must have a website)

## Use Case Description Examples

### 2FA / Security Codes

**GOOD:**
"We send one-time verification codes to users when they log in to their
account from a new device or location. Each code is 6 digits and expires
after 10 minutes. Users must enter this code to complete login. We send
approximately 2-3 codes per user per month."

**BAD:**
"We send codes to users."

---

### Appointment Reminders

**GOOD:**
"Our dental practice sends appointment reminders to patients 24 hours before
their scheduled appointments. Messages include the appointment date, time,
dentist name, and office location. Patients can reply YES to confirm or
RESCHEDULE to change their appointment. We send approximately 1-2 messages
per patient per appointment."

**BAD:**
"Reminder messages."

---

### Order Notifications / E-commerce

**GOOD:**
"We send shipping and delivery updates to customers who place orders on our
e-commerce website. Messages include: order confirmation, shipping notification
with tracking link, out for delivery alert, and delivery confirmation.
Customers receive 3-5 messages per order. All messages include STOP to opt out."

**BAD:**
"Order updates for our store."

---

### Account Notifications / Alerts

**GOOD:**
"Our banking app sends account alerts including: low balance warnings
(when account drops below user-set threshold), large transaction alerts
(purchases over $500), and suspicious activity notifications. Users configure
which alerts they want in their account settings. Average 2-4 messages per
month per user."

**BAD:**
"Bank alerts."

---

### Marketing / Promotions

**GOOD:**
"We send promotional messages to customers who opt-in to our SMS marketing
list. Messages include weekly deals, flash sales, and exclusive subscriber
discounts. We send a maximum of 4 messages per month. All messages clearly
identify our brand and include STOP to unsubscribe. Our opt-in rate is
approximately 15% of website visitors."

**BAD:**
"Promotions and deals."

---

### Customer Service / Support

**GOOD:**
"When customers submit a support ticket, they can opt to receive SMS updates
about their ticket status. We send a message when: ticket is received,
agent responds, and ticket is resolved. Customers can reply to the message
to add comments to their ticket. Average 3-4 messages per support case."

**BAD:**
"Support messages."

## Sample Message Examples

### 2FA Messages

**GOOD:**
"Your Acme verification code is 847291. This code expires in 10 minutes.
If you didn't request this, ignore this message."

"Acme Security: 483920 is your login code. Never share this code with anyone.
We will never call you to ask for this code."

**BAD:**
"Code: 123456" (no context, no branding)
"Your code" (incomplete)

---

### Appointment Messages

**GOOD:**
"Hi Sarah, reminder: Your appointment with Dr. Johnson is tomorrow
(Dec 15) at 2:30 PM at Smile Dental, 123 Main St. Reply YES to confirm
or call (555) 123-4567 to reschedule."

"Acme Auto: Your oil change appointment is scheduled for Friday at 10 AM.
See you at 456 Oak Ave. Questions? Call (555) 987-6543. Reply STOP to
cancel text reminders."

**BAD:**
"You have an appointment tomorrow." (no details)
"Reminder!!!" (no information)

---

### Order / Shipping Messages

**GOOD:**
"Your Acme order #78234 has shipped! Track it here: https://acme.co/track/78234
Expected delivery: Dec 18. Reply STOP to opt out of shipping updates."

"Acme: Your package is out for delivery today and should arrive by 8 PM.
Someone may need to sign. Track: acme.co/t/78234"

**BAD:**
"Your order shipped" (no order number, no tracking)
"Package coming" (too vague)

---

### Marketing Messages

**GOOD:**
"Acme Flash Sale! 40% off all winter items today only. Shop: acme.co/sale
Use code WINTER40. Reply STOP to unsubscribe. Msg&data rates may apply."

"Hi from Acme! As a VIP member, get early access to our Black Friday deals
starting NOW. Shop: acme.co/bf - Reply STOP to opt out"

**BAD:**
"BIG SALE!!!! BUY NOW!!!!" (spammy, no branding, no opt-out)
"Click here for deals" (no branding, no opt-out, vague)

---

### Account Alert Messages

**GOOD:**
"Acme Bank Alert: A $523.45 purchase was made on your card ending 4532 at
Target. If this wasn't you, call (800) 555-1234 immediately."

"Your Acme account balance is below $100. Current balance: $87.32.
Set up auto-reload: acme.co/reload"

**BAD:**
"Alert" (no information)
"Check your account" (vague, could be phishing)

## Opt-In Workflow Description Examples

### Website Signup Opt-In

**GOOD:**
"During account registration on our website, users see a checkbox labeled
'I want to receive SMS updates about my account (optional)' with additional
text below: 'Message and data rates may apply. Approximately 2-4 msgs/month.
Reply STOP to cancel anytime.' The checkbox is unchecked by default.
When checked and form submitted, we store the consent timestamp, user's
IP address, and the exact consent language shown. Users can manage SMS
preferences anytime in their account settings."

**BAD:**
"Users sign up on our website and give us their phone number."

---

### Point of Sale / In-Store Opt-In

**GOOD:**
"At checkout, our staff asks customers if they'd like to receive SMS
receipts and promotions. If yes, the customer enters their phone number
on the PIN pad and is shown the message: 'By entering your number, you
agree to receive SMS from Acme Store. Msg&data rates apply. ~4 msgs/month.
Reply STOP to cancel.' The customer must tap 'I Agree' to proceed. We
store the consent timestamp and store location."

**BAD:**
"We ask for their phone number at the register."

---

### Text-to-Join Opt-In

**GOOD:**
"Customers text JOIN to our shortcode 12345 to subscribe. They immediately
receive a confirmation message: 'Thanks for joining Acme Alerts! You'll
receive ~4 msgs/month with deals and updates. Msg&data rates apply.
Reply HELP for help, STOP to cancel.' We don't send any marketing messages
until they reply YES to confirm. Consent is logged with timestamp and
phone number."

**BAD:**
"They text us to join."

---

### App Download Opt-In

**GOOD:**
"When users download our mobile app and create an account, they're asked
to verify their phone number via SMS. After verification, they see a
screen asking 'Would you like to receive SMS notifications?' with options
for: Order Updates, Promotions, Security Alerts (each toggleable).
Below the options: 'Msg&data rates may apply. You can change these
preferences anytime in Settings.' Users must tap 'Enable' to opt in."

**BAD:**
"They download our app."

---

### Paper Form Opt-In

**GOOD:**
"Patients complete an intake form that includes a section for SMS consent.
The form states: 'I authorize [Practice Name] to send appointment reminders
and health-related information via text message to the phone number provided.
Standard message rates may apply. I understand I can opt out at any time
by texting STOP.' Patients must initial next to this section. We scan and
store the signed consent form digitally with the patient record."

**BAD:**
"They fill out a form with their phone number."

## Screenshot Guidelines

### What to Include

**Registration/Signup Form Screenshots:**
- Full form visible (not cropped)
- SMS consent checkbox clearly visible
- Consent text readable
- Your business name/logo visible
- Submit button showing

**Mobile App Screenshots:**
- Permission dialog visible
- Explanation text readable
- Accept/Decline buttons visible
- App name in header

**Website Popup Screenshots:**
- Full popup visible
- Clear opt-in language
- Privacy link visible
- Close/Submit buttons visible

### What NOT to Submit

- Blurry or low-resolution images
- Screenshots with consent text cropped out
- Mock-ups or designs (must be live implementation)
- Screenshots from other companies
- Marketing materials without opt-in mechanism

---

## Quick Checklist Before Submitting

[ ] Business name matches exactly what's on government registration
[ ] Website is live and shows your business name
[ ] Business registration number (EIN/DUNS) is correct format
[ ] Contact email matches your website domain (recommended)
[ ] Use case description is specific (who, what, when, why)
[ ] Sample messages are realistic and include opt-out for marketing
[ ] Opt-in description explains how users consent and what they see
[ ] Screenshots clearly show consent language
[ ] Privacy policy URL works and mentions SMS
[ ] All information is truthful and accurate

## Industry-Specific Tips

### Healthcare
- HIPAA compliance must be mentioned in opt-in
- No PHI (protected health information) in sample messages
- Mention patient portal or secure messaging for sensitive info

### Financial Services
- Include fraud alert and security notification examples
- Mention compliance with financial regulations
- Show how users manage alert preferences

### E-commerce
- Include full order lifecycle (confirmation, shipped, delivered)
- Show tracking URL format
- Include return/exchange notification if applicable

### Real Estate
- Property showing reminders
- Open house notifications
- Document signing reminders
- New listing alerts for subscribed buyers

### Restaurants / Food Service
- Order ready notifications
- Reservation confirmations
- Waitlist updates
- Loyalty program messages
